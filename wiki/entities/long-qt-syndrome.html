Long QT Syndrome

Long QT Syndrome (LQTS)

Details

Long QT syndrome is the most prevalent cardiac channelopathy, characterized by prolongation of the QT interval on ECG (QTc >470 ms in diagnostic studies) due to abnormal cardiac repolarization. It predisposes patients to torsades de pointes, ventricular fibrillation, and sudden cardiac death without structural heart disease.

Key Facts

  • Prevalence: ~1:2500 based on ECG/genetic screening; possibly as high as 1:2000 accounting for silent carriers; genotype-based estimates suggest up to 1:80. (sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025)

  • 17 subtypes defined by gene mutations; the three major subtypes are LQT1 (KCNQ1, 30–35%), LQT2 (KCNH2, 25–30%), and LQT3 (SCN5A, 5–10%). (sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025)

  • Mechanism: Loss-of-function in KCNQ1/KCNH2 reduces repolarizing K⁺ currents (IKs/IKr); gain-of-function in SCN5A causes persistent Na⁺ influx — both delay repolarization and prolong QT. (sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025)

  • Notable subtypes: Timothy syndrome (LQT8, CACNA1C, gain-of-function, rare and highly malignant); Jervell and Lange-Nielsen syndrome (KCNQ1/KCNE1, autosomal recessive, sensorineural deafness); Andersen-Tawil syndrome (LQT7, KCNJ2, periodic paralysis + arrhythmias + dysmorphic features). (sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025)

  • Arrhythmic risk is highest in childhood, decreases with age; patients >60 yrs have attenuated risk. (sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025)

  • Sex-based risk: Males aged 10–12 have 4× higher risk than females; from ages 18–40, women have 11% vs. 2% risk in men. Sex hormones modulate IKr: estrogen inhibits (↑QT), testosterone potentiates (↓QT). Postpartum period carries elevated risk. (sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025)

  • Diagnosis: 12-lead ECG, Schwartz Score, exercise treadmill test, 24-hr ambulatory ECG; genetic testing for high clinical suspicion with negative workup. Transmembrane pore variants in KCNH2 carry the greatest relative cardiac event risk. (sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025)

  • Management: Nonselective β-blockers (nadolol preferred) → LCSD + mexiletine → ICD. Post-LCSD QTc <500 ms predicts success; QTc >500 ms persistent = ICD candidate. ICD for secondary prevention after aborted cardiac arrest in all LQTS patients. (sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025)

  • Emerging: Lumacaftor (phase II trial NCT04581408) to rescue LQT2 phenotype; high-throughput patch-clamp for VUS evaluation; iPSC-CM + CRISPR for variant-specific drug testing. (sources/channelopathies-jaha-2025)

  • SupRep gene therapy (AAV9, shRNA + shRNA-immune cDNA) validated for LQT1: normalized QTi/APD90 in transgenic rabbits and restored β-adrenergic response (Bains 2024; Dotzler 2023). (sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025)

  • SupRep for LQT2 (KCNH2): normalized QTc from 470 → 414 ms, suppressed EADs and TdP inducibility in rabbit model (Bains 2023). (sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025)

  • LQT3 base editing (ABE8e-SpRY via dual AAV9): corrected SCN5A-M1875T in mice with 54% editing efficiency; normalized QTc and APD90; reduced late INa 66%; ~20% editing sufficient to prevent arrhythmias (Qi 2024). (sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025)

  • Allele-specific RNAi for KCNQ1/KCNH2 is limited by extreme mutational heterogeneity — SupRep's mutation-agnostic approach overcomes this. (sources/gene-therapy-arrhythmia-2025)

  • ESC 2022 guideline recommendations:

    • Diagnose LQTS with QTc ≥480 ms (repeated ECGs), OR Schwartz score ≥3, OR pathogenic mutation irrespective of QT duration: all Class I. (sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022)
    • Non-selective beta-blockers (nadolol or propranolol) for QT-prolonging LQTS: Class I. (sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022)
    • Mexiletine in LQT3 with prolonged QT: Class I (new 2022 upgrade from IIa). (sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022)
    • ICD + beta-blockers after cardiac arrest: Class I; ICD if symptomatic on beta-blockers + genotype-specific therapies: Class I. (sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022)
    • LCSD (Class I): When ICD is contraindicated/declined, OR patient on full therapy with ICD still has multiple shocks or syncope from VA. (sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022)
    • 1-2-3 LQTS Risk calculator to estimate arrhythmic risk before therapy initiation: Class IIa (new). (sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022)
    • ICD in asymptomatic high-risk LQTS (per 1-2-3 LQTS Risk calculator) in addition to genotype-specific therapies: Class IIb. (sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022)
    • Epinephrine challenge for routine LQTS diagnosis: NOT recommended (Class III). EPS: NOT recommended (Class III). (sources/VA-SCD-ESC-2022)
