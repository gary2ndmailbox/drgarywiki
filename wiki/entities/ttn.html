TTN (Titin)

TTN (Titin)

Details

TTN encodes titin, the largest protein in the human body (~3.7 MDa), spanning the entire half-sarcomere from Z-disc to M-line. Titin acts as a molecular spring governing passive myocardial stiffness and as a scaffold for sarcomere assembly. Rare loss-of-function (truncating) variants in TTN are the most common monogenic cause of dilated cardiomyopathy (DCM) and the most frequently identified disease-associated variants in patients with early-onset atrial fibrillation.

Key Facts

  • DCM association: TTN truncating variants (TTNtv) account for ~25% of familial DCM and ~18% of sporadic DCM — the single most common monogenic cause of DCM. Loss-of-function is the established disease mechanism; missense variants in TTN are generally not reported clinically due to high prevalence and uncertain significance.

  • Early-onset AF: In 1293 patients with early-onset AF (diagnosed before age 66) who underwent whole-genome sequencing, TTN harbored the most P/LP variants: 38 participants (27% of all disease-associated variants). Median age at AF diagnosis was 44 years (IQR 36–55). This extends prior work (Choi 2018) finding TTN loss-of-function in 2.1% of all early-onset AF and 6.5% in those diagnosed before age 30. (sources/eoaf-jama-2021)

  • VUS burden: 98 TTN loss-of-function VUSs were identified in the same cohort (the most of any gene); 494 TTN missense variants were excluded from reporting per clinical convention — illustrating how transcript length inflates VUS count. (sources/eoaf-jama-2021)

  • Syndrome classification: Variants assigned predominantly to the DCM category; some overlap with AC/ARVC category. TTN is on both cardiomyopathy and arrhythmia gene panels of commercial testing laboratories. (sources/eoaf-jama-2021)

  • Atrial myopathy hypothesis: TTN truncating variants in early-onset AF may drive disease via atrial structural dysfunction (atrial myopathy) rather than primary ion channel dysfunction — consistent with AF as an initial cardiomyopathy manifestation. (sources/eoaf-jama-2021)

  • TTN P/LP variants in AF <40 without structural disease: In a prospective cohort of 122 patients with AF before age 40 specifically excluding structural heart disease, 4 of 8 P/LP variant carriers had TTN variants — making TTN the most common cardiomyopathy gene identified. CMR findings were normal in the cohort. This corroborates the "atrial myopathy" model: TTN loss-of-function can cause clinically isolated AF in young patients before overt cardiomyopathy develops. (sources/eoaf-riskfactor-ehj-2026, rating: medium)

  • UK Biobank population-level AF association (n = 403,990): In the largest WES-based gene-burden study of AF to date, TTN pLOF variants were the strongest and most prevalent rare genetic risk factor for AF. TTN (PSI90 — constitutively expressed cardiac exons) had an OR of 3.85 (95% CI 3.25–4.55; P = 1.09 × 10⁻⁵⁵); TTN cardiac isoforms (N2BA/N2B) OR 2.17; all TTN pLOF variants OR 1.77. Restricting to constitutively spliced-in cardiac exons markedly amplifies signal by excluding non-cardiac isoform variants. (sources/Biobank-AF-JAMA-2024, rating: high)

  • TTN pLOF drives cardiomyopathy risk in AF patients: In cause-specific Cox regression treating AF, HF, and cardiomyopathy as competing events, pLOF variants in the 5 replicated AF genes were associated with cardiomyopathy (HR 3.13, 95% CI 2.24–4.36) and HF (HR 1.51) prior to AF diagnosis, and cardiomyopathy post-AF diagnosis (HR 2.98). When TTN variants were excluded, these CM and HF associations became non-significant — confirming that TTNtv is the primary driver of the cardiomyopathy progression signal, not other AF-associated pLOF genes. (sources/Biobank-AF-JAMA-2024, rating: high)

  • AF PRS does not predict cardiomyopathy: The common genetic AF PRS (per SD increase, OR 1.53 for AF) was not associated with cardiomyopathy (HR 0.99) or HF (HR 1.02) — confirming that rare TTN pLOF variants and common AF-PRS represent biologically distinct pathways: one cardiomyopathy-linked, one arrhythmia-substrate-specific. (sources/Biobank-AF-JAMA-2024, rating: high)

  • DCM — most common gene: TTNtv account for ~25% of familial DCM and ~18% of sporadic DCM, making TTN the most common monogenic cause of DCM. (sources/esc-cmp-2023)

  • Second hit theory: TTNtv confer increased risk for cardiomyopathy when combined with environmental stressors — alcoholic CMP (13.5% TTNtv prevalence vs. 2.9% controls), cancer therapy-induced CMP (7.5% of cases), and peripartum CMP (10% of patients). (sources/esc-cmp-2023)

  • Heterogeneous CMR pattern in NDLVC: TTN genotype in NDLVC shows heterogeneous LGE without the characteristic ring-like pattern of DSP/FLNC/PLN; often associated with lower LVEF. (sources/esc-cmp-2023)

  • ESC 2021 HF Guidelines — TTN in DCM genetic panel and gene-specific outcomes: ESC 2021 identifies TTN as a required gene in the minimum genetic panel for DCM/HNDC (alongside LMNA, MYH7, MYPC, TNNT, RBM20, PLN, SCN5a, BAG3, and others). Gene-specific outcomes reported for TTN-related DCM: (1) higher rate of LV reverse remodeling (up to 70% of TTN-related DCM patients achieve LVEF improvement on standard HF therapy), and (2) higher risk of both atrial and ventricular tachyarrhythmias compared to other DCM genotypes — a combination not observed in most other genetic subtypes. TRED-HF pilot data (referenced in ESC 2021) showed 44% relapse within 6 months after drug withdrawal in non-ischaemic DCM that achieved partial/complete LVEF recovery — relevant caution for TTN patients who transiently improve. (sources/HF-ESC-2021, rating: very high)

Contradictions / Open Questions

  • TTN LV reverse remodeling vs. arrhythmia risk paradox: ESC 2021 reports that TTN-associated DCM has both the highest LV reverse remodeling rate (up to 70%) and higher atrial/ventricular arrhythmia risk. This creates a clinical paradox where a genotype with favourable structural response still warrants heightened arrhythmia vigilance. Whether the reverse remodelers have lower arrhythmia burden is not characterized. (sources/HF-ESC-2021)
  • TTN missense variants excluded from clinical reporting — diagnostic gap: TTN missense variants are routinely excluded from clinical reporting due to their high prevalence in healthy individuals and generally uncertain significance. However, this exclusion policy may miss rare pathogenic missense variants that genuinely contribute to disease. The trade-off between minimizing VUS burden and detecting true pathogenic missense variants in TTN is unresolved; there is no validated functional test to distinguish benign from pathogenic TTN missense variants in routine clinical practice. (sources/eoaf-jama-2021)
  • Second hit theory — penetrance context-dependent, counseling complexity: TTNtv confer substantially elevated DCM risk when combined with environmental stressors (alcohol: 13.5% TTNtv prevalence; cancer therapy: 7.5%; peripartum: 10%). This means a TTNtv carrier who avoids these triggers may never develop clinical disease, while a carrier who develops alcoholic CMP or receives cardiotoxic chemotherapy faces substantially higher risk. Counseling TTNtv carriers about absolute vs. conditional risk — especially for decisions about alcohol use, pregnancy, or chemotherapy — requires personalized communication not currently standardized in guidelines. (sources/esc-cmp-2023)
  • TTN VUS inflation by transcript length — 98 loss-of-function VUSs in a single cohort: TTN harbored the largest VUS count (98 loss-of-function VUSs) in the early-onset AF cohort, partly because transcript length mechanically inflates variant count. The clinical management of a TTN loss-of-function VUS in an AF patient is undefined — no guideline currently recommends genotype-specific management for TTN VUS carriers beyond standard AF care. (sources/eoaf-jama-2021)
