Overview

This contemporary review covers the six major cardiac channelopathies — LQTS, SQTS, Brugada syndrome, early repolarization syndrome, CPVT, and IVF — which are inherited primary electrical disorders of the heart without structural defects. Together they account for >50% of sudden cardiac death (SCD) in individuals under 50. The paper reviews the molecular and genetic mechanisms, diagnostic approaches (including scoring systems and genetic testing), and management strategies for each condition. Emerging therapeutics — including iPSC models, CRISPR/Cas9, RNA-based therapy, and AI diagnostics — are also discussed.

cardiac electrophysiology, channelopathies, heart rhythm disorders, inherited arrhythmias, sudden cardiac death

Key Takeaways

  • Channelopathies cause >50% of SCD in individuals aged <50 years; SCD kills 250,000–300,000 Americans annually
  • LQTS is the most prevalent channelopathy (~1:2500); 17 subtypes defined; top genes are KCNQ1 (LQT1), KCNH2 (LQT2), SCN5A (LQT3); nadolol is preferred β-blocker
  • SQTS prevalence 0.02–0.1%; male-predominant; gain-of-function mutations in K⁺ channel genes (KCNH2, KCNQ1, KCNJ2); ICD first-line, quinidine pharmacologically
  • Brugada syndrome prevalence ~3–5/10,000; 8–10× more common in males; SCN5A loss-of-function in 15–30% of cases; type 1 coved ECG pattern is diagnostic; ICD/quinidine/radiofrequency ablation
  • ERS was previously considered benign; prevalence ~5.8%; KCNJ8 gain-of-function most studied; managed with ICD, quinidine, isoproterenol, PDE-III inhibitors
  • CPVT affects ~1:10,000; RYR2 gain-of-function mutations in 60–70%; exercise-triggered bidirectional VT; β-blockers are cornerstone; LCSD for refractory disease
  • IVF is a diagnosis of exclusion; Purkinje-fiber R-on-T PVCs; DPP6 haplotype elevated >20× in IVF vs. controls; quinidine and ICD are mainstay treatments
  • Sex hormones modulate arrhythmia risk in LQTS: estrogen inhibits IKr (increases QT), testosterone reduces QT; postpartum period carries elevated risk
  • Scoring systems (Schwartz for LQTS/SQTS, Shanghai Score for BrS/ERS) standardize diagnosis
  • iPSC-derived cardiomyocytes, CRISPR/Cas9, and in silico models are transforming precision medicine for channelopathies
  • BrAID trial (NCT04641585) applies machine learning and transcriptomics to BrS diagnosis
  • CASQ2-targeted gene therapy is under active exploration for CPVT

Limitations of the Document

  • The link between specific genetic mutations and SCD risk remains incompletely understood; genotype-phenotype associations are not fully established for several subtypes
  • BrS pathophysiology remains controversial: depolarization vs. repolarization hypothesis vs. neural crest theory
  • IVF has no strict diagnostic workup; guideline recommendations are general
  • Amiodarone and sotalol are ineffective in SQTS1 despite being IKr blockers — ion channel mutations appear to alter drug-channel interactions
  • Risk stratification in asymptomatic BrS and ERS patients remains an open clinical challenge
